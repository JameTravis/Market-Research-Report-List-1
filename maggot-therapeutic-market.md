<p><p>Maggot therapy, also known as larval therapy, is a method of wound healing that involves the controlled application of sterile maggots to wounds. These maggots feed on the dead tissue and bacteria present in the wound, promoting healing and reducing the risk of infection. This therapy has been used for centuries but gained renewed interest in recent years due to its effectiveness and cost-efficiency.</p><p>The global maggot therapeutic market is set to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of chronic wounds, such as diabetic foot ulcers, venous leg ulcers, and pressure ulcers, is one of the primary drivers of this market. Maggot therapy has proven to be highly effective in treating these hard-to-heal wounds, driving the demand for this treatment option.</p><p>Additionally, the rising geriatric population, with its higher vulnerability to chronic wounds, is expected to contribute to the market's growth. Furthermore, the growing preference for non-invasive and minimally invasive wound management techniques, along with the increasing adoption of maggot therapy in hospitals and wound care centers, is driving market expansion.</p><p>The market is also supported by advancements in technology, such as the use of artificial intelligence and robotics in maggot production and application.
