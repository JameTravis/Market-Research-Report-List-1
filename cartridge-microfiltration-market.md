<p><p>Cartridge microfiltration is a popular filtration technique employed in various industries to separate suspended solids from liquid streams. The market for cartridge microfiltration includes different types of cartridges based on the filtration material used. PP (Polypropylene) type cartridges are made of a thermoplastic polymer and are widely used due to their excellent chemical resistance. PES (Polyethersulfone) type cartridges offer high heat resistance and low protein binding, making them suitable for the pharmaceutical and food industries. PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) type cartridges are known for their superior chemical resistance and are used in aggressive chemical applications. Other types of cartridges include PVDF (Polyvinylidene fluoride) and Nylon, catering to specific filtration requirements.</p></p>
